# Framing Document

A frame is the why before the how.

The job is not to summarize a conversation. The job is to identify the problem worth solving now, the outcome that matters, and the boundaries that keep later shaping honest.

---

## Inputs

Use one or more of these:
- transcript
- meeting notes
- stakeholder message
- email thread
- support tickets
- field research notes

Preserve the original source material separately from your interpretation.

---

## Output Structure

### Source
Capture the key raw material that justifies the frame.

Use short quotes, paraphrases with attribution, or distilled observations tied to the source.

### Problem
State what is broken, costly, risky, or blocked.

Use bullets. Keep them specific.

### Outcome
State what should be true if the work succeeds.

Keep outcomes high level and solution-agnostic.

### Boundaries
Optional but often useful.

Spell out what this work is less about and more about so later shaping does not drift into the wrong problem.

---

## Discipline

For each problem or outcome statement, ask:
- what in the source supports this?
- is this a fact, an inference, or a leap?
- did I accidentally slip a solution into the frame?

Cut anything you cannot support.

---

## Minimal Template

```markdown
---

# [Topic] — Frame

- [Quote, note, or attributed observation]

- ...

- ...

- ...
```
